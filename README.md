**Description**: 

Our game explores Dyspraxia, a condition that affects physical coordination and motor planning. Instead of creating a traditional educational experience, we designed an entertainment-focused game where core mechanics simulate the challenge of managing multiple physical actions simultaneously.

In the game, players take on the role of a small octopus barista managing drink orders at a coffee counter. Each drink requires multiple ingredients located at different stations around the screen. Players click and drag floating tentacles to the correct stations to begin tasks. Each station activates a continuous input challenge (e.g., holding a key, tapping repeatedly, balancing a meter, or matching a rhythm). As new steps are introduced, previous challenges remain active, requiring players to coordinate multiple actions at once. Players must also carefully manage tentacle movement, as crossing them can cause tangling, temporarily interrupting progress.

To support onboarding, the game begins with a tutorial level without stress or tangling mechanics, allowing players to learn controls in a low-pressure environment. As players progress through levels, additional mechanics such as stress and tangling are gradually introduced, increasing coordination difficulty. This progression is designed to evoke the feeling of “knowing what to do, but struggling to coordinate everything at once,” reflecting real-world motor-planning challenges experienced by individuals with dyspraxia.

**Setup and Interaction Instructions**:

- Click and drag tentacles to stations to start tasks
- Complete station-specific input challenges (hold, tap, balance, rhythm)
- Manage multiple active challenges simultaneously
- Avoid crossing tentacles to prevent tangling
- Press R to recover when tangled or under high stress

  - Level 3: +30 per order, +50 per level
  - Level 4: +40 per order, +50 per level
  - −5 points per tangling mistake
